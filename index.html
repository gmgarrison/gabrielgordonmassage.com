The Benefits of Massage

In this age of constant planning and lightning-fast decision making, it’s important to carve some time out for ourselves. The body and mind are incredible tools we are utilizing constantly, so naturally, they can experience some serious exhaustion. Even seemingly routine tasks can take remarkable amounts of effort, and before we know it, our energy is drained and our patience and mindfulness go out the window. This is where massage comes in.

Massage can help us prepare for, and recover from, the exertions of everyday life.

The connectedness of hands-on bodywork helps to facilitate the mind/body relaxation and recuperation that is so important for our day-to-day wellness. Healthy and positive therapeutic touch makes way for more efficient function of all systems in the body, as well as calm in the mind.

Table Massage

​This work is performed on a standard massage table with sheets and a blanket. Various modalities can be used throughout the session based on what the client hopes to achieve both in the short-term and in accordance with their long-term goals. No matter what the depth of pressure, the intention of the work is to always be relaxing and rejuvenating!

Thai Massage

Thai Massage, often referred to as Thai Yoga Massage, is a form of bodywork that focuses on opening blocked channels within the body. This is achieved through deep compressions and yoga-esque stretching, all facilitated by the therapist. The therapist can utilize thumbs, palms, forearms, knees or feet depending on the area of the body that needs attention.
